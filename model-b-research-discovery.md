# Model B — Research & Discovery Activities

## About this model

Model B covers activities where PhoenixDX gathers primary data directly from users, stakeholders, or the real-world environment. A key characteristic of this model is that timeline is partially outside the team's control — it depends on other humans showing up, responding, or granting access.

**Phase structure:** Recruitment & Scheduling → Preparation → Facilitation → Synthesis → Report & Playback

**When this model applies:** The team needs to understand real people — their behaviours, motivations, mental models, emotions, or work contexts — before design decisions can be made with confidence.

**Critical planning rule:** Recruitment and Scheduling must always be shown as a separate phase in the Gantt. It is passive calendar time that does not consume design days but directly affects the delivery date. Never bury recruitment time inside Facilitation.

**AI acceleration note:** AI can compress Preparation (guide writing, screener drafting) and Synthesis (thematic clustering, open-text analysis). Facilitation always requires human presence. Recruitment cannot be meaningfully accelerated by AI.

---

## Activities in this model

- User & Stakeholder Interviews
- Surveys
- Contextual Inquiry
- Jobs-to-be-Done
- Empathy Maps
- Google HEART Framework

## User & Stakeholder Interviews

**Double Diamond phase:** Discover
**Model:** B — Research & Discovery
**PhoenixDX approach:** Interviews are the Inquiry Phase. Stakeholders provide business constraints. Users provide functional truth. Balancing these two perspectives is the primary goal. In Large and XL projects, always involve a Silent Observer from the client's internal team in at least 20% of sessions — hearing a user struggle first-hand is more persuasive than any report.

### Effort by size

| Size | Scope trigger | Effort | AI-accelerated | Friction |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Small | 3 stakeholders + 5 users, warm leads | 4.5d | 3.5d | 1.1x |
| Medium | 5 stakeholders + 10 users, tailored guides | 10d | 8d | 1.2x |
| Large | 8 stakeholders + 20 users, segmented personas | 21d | 17d | 1.3x |
| Extra Large | 15+ stakeholders + 40+ users, global regions | 40d | 33d | 1.5x |

### Phase breakdown

**Recruitment & Scheduling**
- Small: 0.5d — 3 stakeholders + 5 users, internal warm leads
- Medium: 1d — 5 stakeholders + 10 users, basic screener for external users
- Large: 3d — 8 stakeholders + 20 users, segmented personas, paid recruitment
- XL: 6d — 15+ stakeholders + 40+ users, global regions, complex B2B role screening

**Preparation**
- Small: 0.5d — 1 standard discussion guide, 1-pager on project goals
- Medium: 1.5d — 2 tailored guides (User vs Stakeholder), stakeholder map
- Large: 3d — 4+ tailored guides, research plan, specialised stimulus materials
- XL: 5d — Multi-language guides, cross-departmental alignment on research objectives

**Facilitation**
- Small: 2d — 8 x 45-min sessions, remote video calls, basic note-taking
- Medium: 4d — 15 x 60-min sessions, recorded and transcribed
- Large: 8d — 25+ sessions, mixed moderated/unmoderated, multiple interviewers
- XL: 15d — 50+ sessions, on-site visits, deep-dive ethnographic focus

**Synthesis**
- Small: 1d — Basic Top 5 themes, identify common pain points
- Medium: 2.5d — Thematic clustering (Affinity Mapping), I-statements, quote selection
- Large: 5d — Multi-dimensional synthesis, mapping Stakeholder Desires vs User Reality
- XL: 10d — Complex pattern analysis, cross-segment variance reports, high-fidelity data coding

**Report & Playback**
- Small: 0.5d — Short summary deck, highlight key contradictions
- Medium: 1d — Full research report with Problem Statements and video clips
- Large: 2d — Immersive workshop, A Day in the Life stories, roadmap recommendations
- XL: 4d — Strategic executive readout, board-level alignment on North Star vision

### Deliverables

- **Small:** Interview recordings, transcripts, synthesis document with Top 5 themes and problem statements
- **Medium:** Full research report with problem statements, video highlight clips, and stakeholder contradiction analysis
- **Large:** Immersive workshop, A Day in the Life stories, multi-dimensional synthesis document, and roadmap recommendations
- **XL:** Strategic executive readout, cross-segment variance report, board-level alignment on North Star vision

### Dependencies

- **Small:** Defined research goals, warm participant list, basic discussion guide
- **Medium:** Research plan, tailored stakeholder and user discussion guides, screener, recording consent
- **Large:** Research plan, 4+ tailored guides, specialist stimulus materials, paid recruitment panel
- **XL:** Multi-language discussion guides, cross-departmental stakeholder map, global recruitment agency

### AI validation requirements

- **Small:** Human must analyse sentiment, non-verbal cues, and contextual nuance from session recordings
- **Medium:** Thematic clustering and I-statement extraction must be validated by researcher — AI misses contextual contradiction
- **Large:** Multi-dimensional synthesis mapping Stakeholder Desires vs User Reality requires human judgement throughout
- **XL:** Complex pattern analysis and cross-segment variance reports require senior researcher validation — AI cannot resolve strategic divergence

### Contingency notes

- **Small:** Scheduling delays with stakeholders are common and outside the team's control — build in flex
- **Medium:** Transcription and coding quality affects synthesis time significantly
- **Large:** Strategic divergence between stakeholder and user findings requires additional synthesis and diplomacy time
- **XL:** Global recruitment of niche roles and multi-region scheduling can extend recruitment phase by weeks

### Scope guard rules

- If Legacy Modernisation goal → Interviews are **mandatory** at minimum Medium size
- If no participant list exists at project kick-off → trigger TIMELINE RISK immediately
- If Information Maturity is LOW → apply +25% buffer to Synthesis phase automatically

## Surveys

**Double Diamond phase:** Discover
**Model:** B — Research & Discovery
**PhoenixDX approach:** Surveys quantify the qualitative — turning gut feelings from interviews into statistically significant data. They are not a replacement for interviews. Always run surveys after qualitative research, not instead of it. Response rate and recruitment time are variables that must be shown as passive timeline in the Gantt.

### Effort by size

| Size | Scope trigger | Effort | AI-accelerated | Friction |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Small | ~50 internal users, basic analysis | 3d | 2d | 1.2x |
| Medium | 100–300 segmented users, logic jumps | 6d | 3.5d | 1.2x |
| Large | Paid external panel, complex branching | 12d | 7d | 1.3x |
| Extra Large | Multi-language, legal review, longitudinal | 22d | 13d | 1.4x |

### Phase breakdown

**Recruitment & Scheduling**
- Small: 0.5d — Internal list of ~50 users, simple email blast
- Medium: 1d — Segmented list (100–300 users), setting up incentives/rewards
- Large: 2d — Paid external panel recruitment, managing quotas for different personas
- XL: 4d — Multi-language panels, complex screening logic, high-volume monitoring

**Preparation**
- Small: 1d — Write 10 basic questions, tool setup (Typeform/SurveyMonkey)
- Medium: 1.5d — Write 20 questions, define logic jumps, internal pilot testing
- Large: 3d — Map questions to KPIs, complex branching logic, bias detection audit
- XL: 5d — Scientific survey design, stakeholder approval on phrasing, legal/GDPR review

**Facilitation (Wait Time)**
- Small: 0.25d — Monitor for 3–5 days, mid-week reminder
- Medium: 0.5d — Monitor for 1–2 weeks, re-send to underrepresented segments
- Large: 1d — Manage data quality, clean out bot responses or low-effort entries
- XL: 2d — Active management across time zones, ongoing data integrity checks

**Synthesis**
- Small: 1d — Basic chart generation, identify majority trends
- Medium: 2d — Cross-tabulation, thematic coding of open text
- Large: 4d — Correlation analysis, statistical significance checking, deep sentiment analysis
- XL: 8d — Longitudinal analysis, multi-segment deep dives

**Report & Playback**
- Small: 0.25d — Short email summary with top 3 takeaways
- Medium: 1d — Presentation deck with visual data storytelling and specific quotes
- Large: 2d — Interactive workshop with stakeholders, mapping data to UX improvements
- XL: 3d — Executive report, strategic recommendations based on data-backed risk

### Deliverables

- **Small:** Survey dataset, basic chart outputs, and short email summary with Top 3 takeaways
- **Medium:** Presentation deck with visual data storytelling, cross-tabulation insights, and specific user quotes
- **Large:** Analysis report with correlation findings, sentiment summary, and stakeholder workshop with UX improvement mapping
- **XL:** Executive report, longitudinal comparison data, and strategic recommendations based on data-backed risk assessment

### Dependencies

- **Small:** Defined research objectives, internal user email list, approved survey tool
- **Medium:** Segmented participant list of 100–300 users, incentive budget, survey tool with logic jump capability
- **Large:** Research objectives, paid panel access, incentive plan, bias detection review process
- **XL:** Global participant panels, legal and GDPR review, multi-language translation, longitudinal baseline data

### AI validation requirements

- **Small:** Human review of statistical interpretation and open-text thematic accuracy for top-line findings
- **Medium:** Cross-tabulation conclusions and thematic coding of open-text responses require human validation
- **Large:** Correlation analysis and statistical significance checks must be validated by a researcher or data analyst
- **XL:** Longitudinal analysis and multi-segment deep dives require senior researcher review — AI cannot assess strategic risk implications

### Contingency notes

- **Small:** Low response rates from internal lists can require re-sending and extended wait time
- **Medium:** Underrepresented segments require active management and re-recruitment mid-survey
- **Large:** Bot responses and low-effort entries require data cleaning that adds unexpected synthesis time
- **XL:** Legal and brand review of survey questions in regulated industries can delay launch by days or weeks

### Scope guard rules

- Survey wait time must always appear as passive timeline in the Gantt — it does not consume design days
- If no participant list exists → block survey, flag dependency not met
- If Information Maturity is LOW → increase size by one tier automatically

## Contextual Inquiry

**Double Diamond phase:** Discover
**Model:** B — Research & Discovery
**PhoenixDX approach:** This is the gold standard of research — we observe what people actually do in their real-world environment rather than what they say they do in an interview. The Synthesis phase often results in a Service Blueprint because we see both the Frontstage (what the user does) and the Backstage (what the system does).

### Effort by size

| Size | Scope trigger | Effort | AI-accelerated | Friction |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Small | 2 sessions, internal staff, 1 site | 4d | 3.5d | 1.2x |
| Medium | 5 sessions, single site, mobile recording | 8d | 7d | 1.2x |
| Large | 10 sessions, multiple sites, safety briefings | 17d | 15d | 1.3x |
| Extra Large | 20+ sessions, multi-region, seasonal variations | 33d | 30d | 1.5x |

### Phase breakdown

**Recruitment & Scheduling**
- Small: 0.5d — 2 sessions, internal staff, simple calendar invite
- Medium: 1d — 5 sessions, single site, managing travel logistics for one location
- Large: 2.5d — 10 sessions, multiple sites, background checks/security clearances
- XL: 5d — 20+ sessions, multi-region, managing complex site access and union/legal rules

**Preparation**
- Small: 1d — Basic observation guide, shaking hands with participants
- Medium: 1.5d — Structured Master/Apprentice guide, task list, hardware setup (mobile cams)
- Large: 3d — Deep industry research, safety briefings, specialised recording gear setup
- XL: 5d — Custom data-capture frameworks, cross-functional team briefing, pilot visit

**Facilitation (Fieldwork)**
- Small: 1d — 2 x 2-hour sessions (1 site)
- Medium: 2.5d — 5 x 2-hour sessions, shadowing across different times of day
- Large: 6d — 10+ sessions, multi-day shadowing, capturing artefacts/workarounds
- XL: 12d — Multi-week engagement, capturing seasonal or multi-shift variations

**Synthesis**
- Small: 1d — High-level A-ha moments, documenting top 3 workarounds
- Medium: 2d — Detailed Sequence Models, identifying environment-driven pain points
- Large: 4d — Full Work Models (Flow, Sequence, Cultural, Artefact, Physical), thematic coding
- XL: 8d — Comprehensive workflow blueprints, mapping systemic failures across regions

**Report & Playback**
- Small: 0.5d — Short photo diary and bulleted insight list
- Medium: 1d — Presentation with photos/videos of users in the wild, pain point map
- Large: 1.5d — Immersive workshop, A Day in the Life storytelling, roadmap validation
- XL: 3d — Executive strategic readout, service-level shift recommendations

### Deliverables

- **Small:** Short photo diary, bulleted insight list, and Top 3 workaround documentation
- **Medium:** Presentation with field photos and videos, Sequence Models, pain point map, and site-specific insights
- **Large:** Full Work Models (Flow, Sequence, Cultural, Artefact, Physical), thematic coding report, and immersive workshop
- **XL:** Comprehensive workflow blueprints, systemic failure mapping across regions, and executive strategic readout

### Dependencies

- **Small:** Internal staff access, site permission, basic observation guide, calendar availability for 2 sessions
- **Medium:** Single site access approval, Master/Apprentice observation guide, mobile recording equipment, 5 participant schedule
- **Large:** Multi-site access approvals, safety briefings, specialised recording equipment, 10 participant schedules, background checks
- **XL:** Multi-region access approvals, union or legal clearances, custom data-capture frameworks, 20+ participant coordination

### AI validation requirements

- **Small:** All field observations and photo diary entries must be reviewed by a human researcher for accuracy
- **Medium:** Sequence model analysis and environment-driven pain point identification require human researcher validation
- **Large:** Full Work Model analysis requires senior researcher — AI cannot interpret cultural or physical environment context
- **XL:** Comprehensive workflow blueprint synthesis across regions requires full human research team validation

### Contingency notes

- **Small:** Even internal site visits require scheduling coordination that can delay start
- **Medium:** Travel logistics to a single site add lead time not always factored into estimates
- **Large:** Multi-site security clearances and background checks can add weeks to recruitment phase
- **XL:** Multi-region legal and privacy requirements (GDPR/HIPAA) regarding recording users can delay execution by weeks

### Scope guard rules

- If Legacy Modernisation goal → Contextual Inquiry is **strongly recommended** — removing it triggers STRATEGIC RISK
- Site access must be confirmed before this activity can be scheduled in the Gantt
- If Information Maturity is LOW → Contextual Inquiry is mandatory before wireframing

## Jobs-to-Be-Done

**Double Diamond phase:** Discover
**Model:** B — Research & Discovery
**PhoenixDX approach:** JTBD requires a detective mindset to uncover the specific push and pull forces that trigger a change in behaviour. Unlike standard interviews, we focus on the moment of switching — what made the user hire or fire a product. In B2B environments, the Buyer (who decides) and the Actor (who uses) are often different people and must both be interviewed.

### Effort by size

| Size | Scope trigger | Effort | AI-accelerated | Friction |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Small | 5 recent switchers, 3–5 Job Stories | 3.5d | 2.5d | 0.9x |
| Medium | 8–10 participants, Forces of Progress canvas | 8d | 6d | 0.9x |
| Large | 15–20 participants, Job Maps, opportunity landscape | 17d | 13d | 1.0x |
| Extra Large | 40+ participants, global markets, ODI scoring | 35d | 27d | 1.2x |

### Phase breakdown

**Recruitment & Scheduling**
- Small: 0.5d — 5 participants, recent switchers (bought/signed up in last 30 days)
- Medium: 1d — 8–10 participants, segmented by 2 use-cases, incentive management
- Large: 2.5d — 15–20 participants, multiple personas, complex screening for non-consumers
- XL: 5d — 40+ participants, global markets, recruiting for legacy and future job states

**Preparation**
- Small: 0.5d — Define the Job hypothesis, simple interview guide
- Medium: 1d — Refine Forces of Progress canvas, pilot session
- Large: 2d — Develop multiple Job Maps, align with stakeholder product goals
- XL: 4d — Scientific rigour in screening, cross-functional alignment on JTBD scope

**Facilitation**
- Small: 1d — 5 x 1-hour Deep Dive sessions focused on the purchase timeline
- Medium: 2.5d — 10 sessions, exploring Anxiety and Habit blockers in depth
- Large: 5.5d — 20 sessions, potentially two researchers to cover ground faster
- XL: 12d — Multi-week engagement, capturing jobs across different life stages or business cycles

**Synthesis**
- Small: 1d — Write 3–5 core Job Stories (When I... I want to... So that...)
- Medium: 2.5d — Map the Forces of Progress and Value Proposition canvas
- Large: 5d — Create Job Maps for multiple personas, identify unmet needs/market gaps
- XL: 10d — Comprehensive Opportunity Landscape, quantifying job importance vs satisfaction

**Report & Playback**
- Small: 0.5d — Short deck with Job Stories and top feature recommendations
- Medium: 1d — Presentation of the Forces map, 1-hr workshop on feature prioritisation
- Large: 2d — Interactive Opportunity workshop, roadmap alignment based on JTBD scores
- XL: 4d — Strategic executive readout, board-level alignment on product-market fit

### Deliverables

- **Small:** Core Job Stories document (When I... I want to... So that...) with top feature recommendations
- **Medium:** Forces of Progress canvas, Value Proposition alignment map, and 1-hour feature prioritisation workshop
- **Large:** Prioritised Job Maps across multiple personas, Opportunity Landscape, and roadmap alignment workshop
- **XL:** Comprehensive Opportunity Landscape with ODI importance vs satisfaction scores and strategic executive readout

### Dependencies

- **Small:** Access to 5 recent switchers or new sign-ups, simple interview guide focused on the purchase timeline
- **Medium:** Screener for 2 use-case segments, Forces of Progress canvas template, 8–10 recruited participants
- **Large:** Multiple Job Map frameworks, 15–20 recruited participants across personas, research plan
- **XL:** Global recruitment agency, multi-week engagement plan, ODI survey framework, 40+ participants

### AI validation requirements

- **Small:** Human must validate Job Stories against lived user context — AI cannot interpret emotional and social motivations
- **Medium:** Forces of Progress and Value Proposition mapping require human researcher to validate against interview data
- **Large:** Job Maps and unmet need identification require senior researcher validation — AI cannot distinguish genuine gaps from noise
- **XL:** ODI scoring and Opportunity Landscape require human researcher and product strategist review at XL scale

### Contingency notes

- **Small:** Recruiting recent switchers is time-sensitive — window of recall closes quickly after adoption
- **Medium:** Stakeholder resistance to moving from persona thinking to situational thinking extends playback time
- **Large:** B2B environments require separate Buyer and User interviews which doubles recruitment complexity
- **XL:** Multi-week global engagement with diverse life stages or business cycles is highly susceptible to scheduling disruption

## Empathy Maps

**Double Diamond phase:** Discover (used as bridge into Define)
**Model:** B — Research & Discovery
**PhoenixDX approach:** An Empathy Map is the bridge between raw research data and the Definition phase. It forces the team to look beyond what the user does and consider what they think, feel, see, and hear. Always cross-reference the Empathy Map with the Customer Journey Map in Large and XL projects — if the map shows Anxiety but the journey shows Neutral, you have found a hidden UX opportunity.

### Effort by size

| Size | Scope trigger | Effort | AI-accelerated | Friction |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Small | 1 persona, 3–5 SMEs, 2-hour session | 1.5d | 1d | 0.7x |
| Medium | 2–3 personas, 8–10 stakeholders, 4-hour session | 3.5d | 2.5d | 0.8x |
| Large | 5+ personas, cross-functional, 2-day workshop series | 8d | 5.5d | 0.9x |
| Extra Large | 10+ personas, global regions, Immersive Empathy Summit | 15d | 10.5d | 1.0x |

### Phase breakdown

**Recruitment & Scheduling**
- Small: 0.25d — Identify 3–5 internal subject matter experts (SMEs)
- Medium: 0.5d — Recruit 8–10 diverse internal stakeholders + 2–3 actual users
- Large: 1d — Coordinate cross-functional teams (Product, Sales, Support) for workshops
- XL: 2d — Global stakeholder coordination, managing multiple time zones/regions

**Preparation**
- Small: 0.25d — Simple template setup, gathering basic interview notes
- Medium: 0.5d — Consolidating research from 5–8 interviews into insight snippets
- Large: 1.5d — Deep-dive research audit, preparing Evidence Packs for workshop participants
- XL: 3d — Audit of global research data, segmenting data by region/persona for multiple sessions

**Facilitation**
- Small: 0.25d — 1 x 2-hour session for a single persona
- Medium: 0.5d — 1 x 4-hour session covering 2–3 core personas
- Large: 1.5d — Series of workshops (2 days) covering 5+ personas/segments
- XL: 3d — Multi-day Immersive Empathy Summit, specialised breakout tracks

**Synthesis**
- Small: 0.25d — Digitise post-its, summarise key pains/gains
- Medium: 1d — Resolve contradictions, map Says/Does vs Thinks/Feels gaps
- Large: 2.5d — Root-cause analysis, link empathy insights to specific UI requirements
- XL: 5d — Detailed emotional journey analysis, map empathy shifts across 10+ personas

**Report & Playback**
- Small: 0.25d — Short deck with the digitised Empathy Map
- Medium: 0.5d — Presentation linking empathy data to the Problem Statement
- Large: 1.5d — Stakeholder workshop to prioritise features based on Pain/Gain findings
- XL: 2d — Strategic readout for leadership, aligning brand/product voice with empathy data

### Deliverables

- **Small:** Digitised Empathy Map for one persona with key pains and gains summary
- **Medium:** Presentation linking empathy data to Problem Statement, covering 2–3 personas
- **Large:** Stakeholder workshop output with feature prioritisation based on Pain/Gain findings across 5+ personas
- **XL:** Strategic readout for leadership with brand and product voice alignment across 10+ personas

### Dependencies

- **Small:** Basic interview notes or prior research, 3–5 internal SMEs available for 2 hours
- **Medium:** Consolidated research snippets from 5–8 interviews, 8–10 diverse stakeholders available for 4 hours
- **Large:** Evidence Packs from prior research phases, cross-functional team availability for 2-day series
- **XL:** Global research data audit, multi-region stakeholder coordination, specialised workshop facilitation plan

### AI validation requirements

- **Small:** Human must validate emotional accuracy of Says/Thinks/Does/Feels against source research data
- **Medium:** AI contradiction mapping between Says and Does must be validated by a researcher with access to raw interview data
- **Large:** Root-cause analysis linking empathy insights to specific UI requirements requires senior UX lead validation
- **XL:** Multi-persona emotional journey analysis at global scale requires full human research team review

### Contingency notes

- **Small:** Low UX maturity in SMEs can result in maps filled with assumptions rather than user truths
- **Medium:** Resolving Says vs Does contradictions requires facilitation time that is often underestimated
- **Large:** Cross-functional workshop coordination and Evidence Pack preparation adds significant prep overhead
- **XL:** Global coordination across time zones and cultural contexts significantly extends recruitment and facilitation phases

## Google HEART Framework

**Double Diamond phase:** Discover
**Model:** B — Research & Discovery
**PhoenixDX approach:** We use HEART to move beyond vanity metrics (like total page views) and focus on meaningful UX signals that correlate with business growth. Always include a Task Success deep-dive in the Synthesis phase — define the Critical Paths (the 5 minutes of a user's day where the software must work perfectly) and set baseline metrics for those moments before any design changes are made.

### Effort by size

| Size | Scope trigger | Effort | AI-accelerated | Friction |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Small | 1 product flow, 2–3 stakeholders | 2d | 1.5d | 0.8x |
| Medium | End-to-end product journey, Product/Eng/Data leads | 5d | 3.5d | 0.9x |
| Large | 3+ product squads, multiple modules | 10d | 7d | 1.0x |
| Extra Large | Global enterprise, regional Data and Product heads | 20d | 14d | 1.1x |

### Phase breakdown

**Recruitment & Scheduling**
- Small: 0.25d — Identify 2–3 key Product/Tech stakeholders
- Medium: 0.5d — Coordinate Product, Engineering, and Data Science leads
- Large: 1d — Recruit cross-functional leads across 3+ product squads
- XL: 2d — Global coordination of regional Data and Product heads

**Preparation**
- Small: 0.5d — Audit current basic analytics (e.g. GA4/Hotjar) for 1 feature
- Medium: 1d — Review data logs for a full app, pre-fill Goal hypotheses
- Large: 2d — Deep-dive audit of multi-platform data silos, draft a Metrics Catalog
- XL: 4d — Comprehensive audit of legacy vs new data pipelines, strategic KPI alignment

**Facilitation**
- Small: 0.5d — 1 x 3-hour session to define Goals, Signals, and Metrics for 1 flow
- Medium: 1d — 2 x 3-hour sessions covering the end-to-end product journey
- Large: 2d — Series of HEART Sprints with different feature teams
- XL: 4d — Immersive Metrics Summit to align global product vision and measurement

**Synthesis**
- Small: 0.5d — Finalise a 1-page HEART matrix for the core team
- Medium: 1.5d — Map Signals to specific technical tracking requirements (Events)
- Large: 3d — Create a UX Measurement Plan with tiered priority levels
- XL: 6d — Develop a global Measurement Governance framework, data visualisation specs

**Report & Playback**
- Small: 0.25d — Brief summary deck with the agreed HEART matrix
- Medium: 1d — Presentation of the measurement roadmap and dashboard mockups
- Large: 2d — Stakeholder alignment on Success Benchmarks, ROI mapping
- XL: 4d — Executive readout, long-term data strategy, integration with OKRs

### Deliverables

- **Small:** 1-page HEART matrix for the core team with defined Goals, Signals, and Metrics
- **Medium:** Measurement roadmap, dashboard mockups, and presentation of Success Benchmarks
- **Large:** UX Measurement Plan with tiered priority levels and technical tracking specifications
- **XL:** Global Measurement Governance framework, data visualisation specifications, and OKR integration strategy

### Dependencies

- **Small:** Defined business objectives, access to basic analytics (e.g. GA4), 2–3 key stakeholders
- **Medium:** Product analytics access, Product, Engineering, and Data Science lead availability
- **Large:** Multi-platform data audit, cross-squad stakeholder alignment, analytics platform access
- **XL:** Global data pipeline access, regional Data and Product head availability, OKR documentation

### AI validation requirements

- **Small:** Human must validate that AI-suggested metrics align with actual business KPIs and available data infrastructure
- **Medium:** Signal-to-Event mapping must be reviewed by Engineering to confirm tracking feasibility
- **Large:** UX Measurement Plan priorities require human review — AI cannot assess organisational data maturity or political feasibility
- **XL:** Global governance framework requires executive alignment — AI cannot resolve competing regional measurement priorities

### Contingency notes

- **Small:** Data availability gaps can prevent meaningful metric definition — must be surfaced early
- **Medium:** Misalignment between what is measurable and what stakeholders want measured requires facilitation
- **Large:** Data trapped in departmental silos significantly extends preparation and alignment phases
- **XL:** Legacy data pipeline conflicts and regional measurement inconsistencies can extend XL engagements substantially

### Scope guard rules

- If no analytics platform is confirmed → block HEART, flag dependency not met
- HEART should always precede Usability Testing in the Gantt — establishes the baseline before testing changes anything
