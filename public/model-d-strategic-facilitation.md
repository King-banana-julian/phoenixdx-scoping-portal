# Model D — Strategic Facilitation Activities

## About this model

Model D covers activities where PhoenixDX facilitates structured strategic thinking with senior stakeholders. These activities define the Why and the What before any design or development work begins.

**Phase structure:** Preparation → Workshop Facilitation → Documentation → Strategic Alignment → Sign-off

**When this model applies:** The team needs to establish shared strategic direction — on the business model, the value proposition, or the core problem — before UX or design activities can be scoped with confidence.

**Critical planning rule:** These activities cannot be compressed. The value is in the workshop — the facilitated conversation between people who need to reach agreement. Rushing facilitation produces outputs nobody trusts.

**AI acceleration note:** AI is useful in Preparation (research, canvas setup, brief drafting) and Documentation (digitising and structuring outputs). Workshop Facilitation and Strategic Alignment are always human-led. AI cannot resolve organisational conflict or validate strategic intent.

---

## Activities in this model

- Business Model Canvas
- Value Proposition Canvas
- Problem Framing

## Business Model Canvas

**Double Diamond phase:** Discover
**Model:** D — Strategic Facilitation
**PhoenixDX approach:** The BMC is our Logic Filter. It ensures that UX improvements aren't just aesthetically pleasing but are grounded in Value Propositions the business can sustain and monetise. For Large and XL projects, always pair the BMC with the Value Proposition Canvas — the BMC is too broad to tell you exactly what feature to build.

### Effort by size

| Size | Scope trigger | Effort | AI-accelerated | Friction |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Small | Core founders/leads, 1 x 3-hour session | 1.75d | 1.25d | 0.9x |
| Medium | Cross-functional team, full-day session | 4d | 3d | 1.0x |
| Large | Multi-session series, Current vs Future State | 9d | 6.5d | 1.1x |
| Extra Large | Global strategy sprints, multi-business unit | 18d | 13d | 1.3x |

### Phase breakdown

**Preparation**
- Small: 0.5d — Gather basic mission/vision, set up Miro/Mural board
- Medium: 1d — Audit current revenue streams, internal stakeholder interviews (3–5)
- Large: 2.5d — Deep dive into market data and competitor models, audit cost structures
- XL: 5d — Global ecosystem audit, review multi-region regulatory and supply chain constraints

**Workshop Facilitation**
- Small: 0.5d — 1 x 3-hour session with core founders/leads
- Medium: 1d — 1 full-day session with a cross-functional team (Sales, Product, Ops)
- Large: 2d — Series of workshops (2 days), mapping Current State vs Future State
- XL: 4d — Multi-week Strategy Sprints, global leadership alignment workshops

**Documentation**
- Small: 0.25d — Digitise the canvas with high-level bullet points
- Medium: 0.5d — Refine descriptions, add Assumptions for each of the 9 blocks
- Large: 1.5d — Create a Strategic Narrative, map dependencies between blocks
- XL: 3d — Develop a Business Model Portfolio, detailed financial/operational specs

**Strategic Alignment**
- Small: 0.25d — Brief check-in to ensure the canvas matches the roadmap
- Medium: 1d — Gap analysis: Does our current tech support this Value Prop?
- Large: 2d — Map BMC to OKRs and Product Backlog, identify Innovation Risks
- XL: 4d — Global alignment across business units, resolve resource/priority conflicts

**Sign-off**
- Small: 0.25d — Simple Green Light to proceed with the project
- Medium: 0.5d — Formal presentation to the Executive Sponsor, securing resource buy-in
- Large: 1d — Stakeholder workshop to approve the Strategic North Star
- XL: 2d — Board-level readout, final investment approval for the business case

### Deliverables

- **Small:** Completed BMC artefact with all 9 blocks, basic assumption log, and green light to proceed
- **Medium:** Refined BMC with assumption documentation per block, gap analysis, and executive sponsor presentation
- **Large:** Strategic narrative document, BMC to OKR mapping, innovation risk register, and stakeholder alignment report
- **XL:** Business model portfolio, comprehensive financial and operational specifications, and board-level investment readout

### Dependencies

- **Small:** Mission and vision documents, core team availability for a 3-hour session
- **Medium:** Current revenue stream data, cross-functional team availability, product vision document
- **Large:** Market data, competitor model analysis, cross-department stakeholder availability for 2-day series
- **XL:** Multi-region regulatory data, supply chain documentation, global leadership availability for multi-week sprints

### AI validation requirements

- **Small:** Stakeholder alignment check — AI cannot validate strategic intent or political fit within the organisation
- **Medium:** Gap analysis between current tech and value proposition must be validated by a senior strategist or architect
- **Large:** Strategic narrative and OKR mapping require human review — AI cannot validate organisational readiness or change risk
- **XL:** Cross-regional business model portfolio requires executive sign-off — AI cannot resolve competing regional priorities

### Contingency notes

- **Small:** Misalignment on core value proposition can require additional sessions not initially scoped
- **Medium:** Internal business goal conflicts between departments can extend strategic alignment phase significantly
- **Large:** Dependency mapping between BMC blocks often surfaces hidden risks requiring additional workshops
- **XL:** Cross-regional resource and priority conflicts require diplomatic facilitation that cannot be time-boxed

### Scope guard rules

- BMC is the first activity in any new product or major pivot engagement
- If Product Innovation goal → BMC is **mandatory** before any UX activities begin
- If no executive sponsor is available for sign-off → flag GOVERNANCE RISK

## Value Proposition Canvas

**Double Diamond phase:** Discover
**Model:** D — Strategic Facilitation
**PhoenixDX approach:** We use the VPC to ensure Product-Market Fit. While the BMC answers How the business works, the VPC answers Why the user should care. For Large and XL projects, always use the Adjective Test during Documentation — if your Value Proposition uses generic words like fast, easy, or efficient, it is not specific enough. Push stakeholders to define exactly how (e.g. reduces processing time from 4 days to 4 minutes).

### Effort by size

| Size | Scope trigger | Effort | AI-accelerated | Friction |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Small | 1 primary persona, 1 x 3-hour session | 1.75d | 1.25d | 0.8x |
| Medium | 2–3 segments, Emotional vs Functional Jobs | 4d | 3d | 0.9x |
| Large | 20+ interviews synthesis, Stress Test against competitors | 8.5d | 6d | 1.0x |
| Extra Large | Global Strategy Summits, multi-region value alignment | 17d | 12d | 1.1x |

### Phase breakdown

**Preparation**
- Small: 0.5d — Select 1 primary persona, gather existing feedback
- Medium: 1d — Audit research for 2–3 segments, competitive feature audit
- Large: 2d — Deep-dive research synthesis (20+ interviews), identify Unmet Needs
- XL: 4d — Global market segmentation, audit multi-region competitive landscapes

**Workshop Facilitation**
- Small: 0.5d — 1 x 3-hour session mapping Pain Relievers and Gain Creators
- Medium: 1d — 2 x 3-hour sessions focusing on Emotional vs Functional Jobs
- Large: 2d — Series of workshops, Stress Testing the value prop against competitors
- XL: 4d — Multi-day strategy summits, aligning global product and marketing value-pillars

**Documentation**
- Small: 0.25d — Digitise the canvas, define the Value Statement
- Medium: 1d — Detailed mapping of features to specific Pains/Gains
- Large: 2d — Create a Value Narrative, define messaging hierarchies for Marketing
- XL: 4d — Comprehensive Value Playbook, global brand/product messaging guidelines

**Strategic Alignment**
- Small: 0.25d — Check-in with Product lead to align on the core Hook
- Medium: 0.5d — Gap analysis: Do our features actually solve these high-priority pains?
- Large: 1.5d — Map value pillars to the 12-month roadmap and sales enablement
- XL: 3d — Cross-regional alignment, ensuring value props don't conflict across portfolios

**Sign-off**
- Small: 0.25d — Verbal agreement on the Elevator Pitch
- Medium: 0.5d — Formal presentation to the Product/Marketing heads
- Large: 1d — High-stakes stakeholder workshop, securing Product-Market Fit buy-in
- XL: 2d — Board-level readout, final strategic sign-off for market entry/pivot

### Deliverables

- **Small:** Value Proposition Canvas artefact, value statement, and verbal elevator pitch agreement
- **Medium:** Detailed VPC with feature-to-pain-gain mapping, formal Product and Marketing head presentation
- **Large:** Value Narrative document, competitive positioning report, messaging hierarchy, and Product-Market Fit workshop output
- **XL:** Comprehensive Value Playbook, global brand and product messaging guidelines, and board-level strategic sign-off

### Dependencies

- **Small:** Single primary persona definition, basic customer feedback or research, Product lead availability
- **Medium:** Research synthesis for 2–3 segments, competitive feature audit, Product and Marketing lead availability
- **Large:** Deep research synthesis across 20+ interviews, competitive landscape analysis, 2-day workshop team availability
- **XL:** Global market segmentation data, multi-region competitive landscape, global Product and Marketing leadership availability

### AI validation requirements

- **Small:** Human must validate AI-generated Pain Relievers and Gain Creators against actual user research data
- **Medium:** Feature-to-pain-gain mapping must be reviewed by Product and Marketing leads for strategic accuracy
- **Large:** Value Narrative and competitive positioning require human strategist sign-off — AI cannot validate market fit
- **XL:** Global brand messaging consistency across regions requires executive review — AI cannot resolve competing regional priorities

### Contingency notes

- **Small:** Vague or generic value propositions require additional facilitation to reach specificity — push for measurable statements
- **Medium:** Misalignment between Product and Marketing on core value drivers extends strategic alignment phase
- **Large:** Identifying genuine differentiators in saturated markets requires deeper competitive research than initially scoped
- **XL:** Cross-regional portfolio conflicts and brand cannibalism risk require diplomatic senior leadership facilitation

### Scope guard rules

- VPC is most effective immediately after BMC — should appear as a linked activity in the Gantt
- If Digital Experience goal → VPC is **recommended**
- If entering a new market or pivoting → VPC is **mandatory**

## Problem Framing

**Double Diamond phase:** Define
**Model:** D — Strategic Facilitation
**PhoenixDX approach:** Problem Framing defines the Why before the What. It prevents teams from building a business model around a solution that doesn't have a clear problem to solve. The key failure mode is Solution-First Bias — when a stakeholder has already bought software, the facilitation phase requires significantly more time to un-frame the assumed solution and find the actual problem it was supposed to solve.

### Effort by size

| Size | Scope trigger | Effort | AI-accelerated | Friction |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------|
| Small | 2–3 known pain points, 1 x 3-hour 5 Whys session | 1.75d | 1.25d | 0.9x |
| Medium | Support data audit, Abstraction Ladders, How Might We | 4d | 2.5d | 0.9x |
| Large | 2-day workshop, 3 lenses (User, Biz, Tech) | 8.5d | 6d | 1.0x |
| Extra Large | Multi-week Strategy Sprints, board-level North Star | 16d | 11d | 1.2x |

### Phase breakdown

**Preparation**
- Small: 0.5d — Gather 2–3 known pain points, draft a Problem Statement hypothesis
- Medium: 1d — Audit support logs/data, stakeholder interviews (3–5), competitive landscape
- Large: 2d — Deep-dive research synthesis, map the Problem Ecosystem, cross-dept audit
- XL: 4d — Global market trend analysis, audit macro-economic/regulatory shifts

**Workshop Facilitation**
- Small: 0.5d — 1 x 3-hour 5 Whys session, define the Primary User
- Medium: 1d — 1 full-day session using Abstraction Ladders and How Might We prompts
- Large: 2d — 2-day workshop, Problem Reframing from 3 different lenses (User, Biz, Tech)
- XL: 3d — Multi-week Strategy Sprints, global leadership workshops to align on the North Star

**Documentation**
- Small: 0.25d — Digitise the 1-sentence Problem Statement and the BMC core
- Medium: 0.5d — Create a Problem Brief including constraints and success metrics
- Large: 1.5d — Detailed Problem Canvas or Strategy Map, map dependencies
- XL: 3d — Comprehensive State of the Union report, detailed evidence-based findings

**Strategic Alignment**
- Small: 0.25d — Brief check-in with the Project Owner to verify focus
- Medium: 1d — Map the framed problem to the current Product Vision and OKRs
- Large: 2d — Gap analysis: identify Unmet Needs and Business Risk if the problem isn't solved
- XL: 4d — Cross-regional alignment, ensure the framed problem holds true across markets

**Sign-off**
- Small: 0.25d — Verbal agreement on the problem to be solved
- Medium: 0.5d — Formal presentation to the Steering Committee, securing the Mission
- Large: 1d — High-stakes workshop, securing resource commitment based on the Evidence
- XL: 2d — Board-level readout, final strategic sign-off for the business case/investment

### Deliverables

- **Small:** Single validated Problem Statement, basic constraints log, and verbal agreement to proceed
- **Medium:** Problem Brief with constraints, success metrics, Steering Committee presentation, and Mission sign-off
- **Large:** Problem Canvas or Strategy Map with dependency mapping, Unmet Needs analysis, and resource commitment workshop
- **XL:** State of the Union report, triangulated evidence base, cross-regional problem alignment document, and board-level sign-off

### Dependencies

- **Small:** 2–3 known pain points or stakeholder feedback, Project Owner availability for 3-hour session
- **Medium:** Support logs or analytics data, 3–5 stakeholder interviews, competitive landscape review
- **Large:** Deep research synthesis, multi-department stakeholder availability for 2-day workshop series
- **XL:** Global market trend analysis, multi-region research data, cross-regional leadership availability for multi-week sprints

### AI validation requirements

- **Small:** Human facilitator must confirm the Problem Statement is grounded in evidence and not a disguised solution
- **Medium:** Problem Brief mapping to Product Vision and OKRs requires human strategist review for accuracy and political feasibility
- **Large:** Multi-lens Problem Canvas requires senior facilitator to validate that all three perspectives (User, Biz, Tech) are genuinely reconciled
- **XL:** Cross-regional evidence-based findings require full human research and strategy team review before board presentation

### Contingency notes

- **Small:** Solution-first stakeholders require significant facilitation time to un-frame assumed solutions before genuine problem definition can begin
- **Medium:** Mapping the framed problem to existing OKRs and Product Vision often surfaces misalignment that requires senior resolution
- **Large:** Conflict of interest between departments — where one team's problem is another's job security — requires diplomatic XL facilitation
- **XL:** Cross-regional problem alignment is highly susceptible to political and cultural dynamics that cannot be time-boxed

### Scope guard rules

- Problem Framing must precede all design activities — it defines the problem the design will solve
- If requirements are described as unclear or evolving by the sales rep at intake → Problem Framing is **mandatory**
- If Solution-First Bias is detected at intake (client has already chosen a solution) → flag to delivery lead before scoping begins
